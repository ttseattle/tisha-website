This application supports disaster awareness and recovery in Washington state. An application for "RRAIN" already existed for iOS when I rewrote a version for Android. "RRAIN Washington" is compatabile for phones and tablets and provides resources such as extreme weather alerts taken from an RSS feed, several important web links, and a Google map for Washington traffic.

This project's aim is to make communication with blind people easier by providing a search engine for American Sign Language. Instead of typing in words, "ASL Search" provides a form where you can choose the features of a sign, such as the hand shape, orientation, and movement. Videos of the best matching signs are then listed. I contributed to "ASL Search" as a member of the Mobile Accessibility Lab at UW. This has been an ongoing project for a few years now, and as the latest contributor, I have been working on the search engine results page as well as the overall design of the site.

"Nam Pham Photography" is a portfolio website created for Nam Pham, the brother of one of the members I collaborated with for this project. Because the site was for a client, it was important that it displayed Pham's work in an artistic way fit for a photographer, however was easy to find details in such as contact information. With this in mind, "Nam Pham Photogrpahy" was designed to be clean but stylish, and cool but straightforward.

This web page was inspired by Your Next Five Books, a service offered by the Seattle Public Libraries where the librarians recommend you five fantastic books based on a title that you like. Similarly, if you enter your favorite song into Your Next Five Songs, five new songs will be generated for your liking! These songs are chosen by utilizing the Spotify API and looking at related artists and their top tracks.
